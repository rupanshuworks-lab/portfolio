Situation

Women's Day was approaching and the feed had no planned hero piece — not even a rough direction. For a brand that lives on credibility, showing up with nothing (or with something obviously rushed and generic) was worse than staying quiet.

The problem wasn't "make a reel." It was make a reel that still feels worthy of the day when you're starting from zero narrative, zero footage, and almost no runway.

Real constraint

Moment marketing dies when you're late. The algorithm and the culture move on in hours. So the constraint was time and truth: the story had to be real enough to clear internal and subject approval fast, without a long polish cycle.

"If we wait for perfect conditions, we miss the day entirely. The problem was the calendar — not the ambition."

What had to be solved first

Before hooks, pacing, or captions, the problem was selection: whose story is strong enough to carry the moment without feeling like brand theatre? Then risk: can we get that person to yes quickly? Then execution: can edit and sign-off land while the topic still matters?

Creative only works here if those three locks open in order. Skip one and you're either late or dishonest.
